A smartphone is a handheld device that combines the
functionality of a mobile phone with various features
like internet browsing, email, camera, and apps. It's like
a pocket-sized computer. The term "smartphone"
became popular in the 2000s. While there isn't a single
inventor, companies like Apple, with the iPhone in 2007,
and others like BlackBerry and Nokia, played key roles in
popularizing and advancing smartphone technology.





▶







The World Wide Web (WWW) is a system of inter
connected documents and resources linked by hyperlinks
and URLs, accessible via the internet. It allows people to
share and access information, images, and other content.
The World Wide Web was invented by Sir Tim Berners-Lee
in 1989. He developed the first web browser and editor,
creating the foundation for websites and the modern
internet as we know it.





▶







Chuck Hull invented 3D printing in the 1980s
introducing a method of creating three-dimensional
objects layer by layer. This technology has applications
in various fields, from manufacturing to healthcare.





▶







The Global Positioning System (GPS), developed in
the 1970s by the United States, uses satellites
to provide accurate location information. It has become
essential for navigation and mapping.





▶







The personal computer, with pioneers like Steve
Jobs and Bill Gates, gained popularity in the 1970s.
It brought computing power to individuals and laid
the foundation for the digital age.





▶







Magnetic Resonance Imaging. Invented in 1977 by
Paul Lauterbur and Sir Peter Mansfield, MRI is a
medical imaging technology that uses magnets
and radio waves to create detailed images of the
inside of the body, aiding in diagnosis and treatment.





▶







Fiber optic communications involve transmitting
information through thin strands of glass or plastic using
light pulses. It was developed in the 1970s.





▶







Email, short for electronic mail, was invented by Ray
Tomlinson in 1971. It allows people to send messages
electronically over computer networks.





▶







In 1971, the microprocessor, a tiny chip that
acts as the brain of a computer, was
invented by Ted Hoff and Federico Faggin.





▶







Developed in the 1960s, the Apollo Guidance Computer
was a key technology for NASA's Apollo missions to the
moon, helping spacecraft navigate and control.





▶







In 1958, Arthur Schawlow and Charles Townes
invented the laser, a device that produces a
narrow, intense beam of light with various
applications in industry, medicine, and communications.





▶







The transistor, invented by John Bardeen, Walter
Brattain, and William Shockley in 1947, is a tiny
electronic device that has become a fundamental
building block of modern electronics.





▶







The jet engine, designed in the 1930s by Frank Whittle
and Hans von Ohain independently, revolutionized air
travel by providing a more efficient and powerful
means of propulsion.

Discovered by Alexander Fleming in 1928,
penicillin is the first widely used antibiotic,
revolutionizing medicine by effectively treating
bacterial infections.

In the late 19th century, inventors like Guglielmo
Marconi and Nikola Tesla contributed to the
development of radio, enabling wireless communication.
